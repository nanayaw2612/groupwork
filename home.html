WATER POLLUTION

On the 18th of November, LYNCTEL @NETwent around the nation to report about how illegal mining activites is destroying our water bodies.
In our current situation now, for about 80% of the water bodies in Ghana is polluted due to the ilegal minning activities.

REGIONS INVOLVED IN THE WATER POLLUTION

  • Eastern Region
  • Ashanti Rerion
  • Western Region
  • Central Region
  • Western North Region
  • Brong-Ahafo (Bono) Region
  • Northern Region
  • North East Region

Eastern Region: The Eastern Region, especially in areas like Akyem and Nsutam, also faces significant challenges with illegal mining.
The activities here often involve small-scale miners who work without permits,
leading to environmental degradation and loss of farmland.

Ashanti Region: The Ashanti Region is one of the major hotspots for illegal mining.
It is home to both legal and illegal gold mining operations, especially in districts like Obuasi, where gold deposits are abundant.
Many illegal miners work in and around these areas, often engaging in small-scale mining without the proper licenses.

Western Region: The Western Region, particularly the Western North and Western South districts, has been heavily affected by illegal mining activities.
Towns such as Prestea, Tarkwa, and the surrounding areas have seen widespread illegal mining.
These regions are rich in gold resources,
making them prime locations for both legal and illegal mining

Central Region: In the Central Region, illegal mining is concentrated in areas like the Upper Denkyira West and Upper Denkyira East districts.
The region has experienced an increase in illegal mining activities, particularly in gold mining.

Western North Region Located in the southwestern part of Ghana, the Western North Region has also become a major site for illegal mining.
The region's gold-rich areas like Sefwi Wiawso have attracted numerous illegal miners, resulting in deforestation,
water contamination, and other environmental issues.

Brong-Ahafo(Bono) Region: The Brong-Ahafo region, now divided into Bono, Bono East, and Ahafo regions, has witnessed significant illegal mining activities,
particularly in districts like Kenyasi.
Illegal miners have flocked to these areas due to the abundance of gold.

Region (and North East Region): While less commonly associated with illegal mining compared to the southern regions,
parts of the Northern Region, especially around the Bole and Mamprusi areas,
have seen some illegal mining activities, particularly for gold.
